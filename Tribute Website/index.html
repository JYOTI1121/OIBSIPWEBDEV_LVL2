Rani Lakshmi Bai

1828 - 1858

Rani Lakshmi Bai (aka Jhansi ki Rani / Rani of Jhansi)

  • Early Life

    • Rani Lakshmibai was born on 19 November 1828 in the town of Varanasi into a Marathi Karhade Brahmin family. She was named Manikarnika and was called Manu. She was taught to read and write and was skilled in Shooting, Horsemanship and Fencing.

    Nana Sahib and Tantia Tope were her childhood friends.

  • Marriage

    • Manikarnika was married to the Maharaja of Jhansi, Gangadhar Rao Newalkar, in May 1842 and was afterwards called Lakshmibai / Laxmibai in honour of the Hindu Goddess Devi Lakshmi (according to the Maharashtrian tradition of women being given a new name after marriage). In September 1851, she gave birth to a son named Damodar Rao who died 4 months after birth. The maharaja then adopted his cousin's son Anand Rao and named him Damodar Rao.
    After Maharaja's death, the britishers rejected Damodar's claim to the throne since he was adopted and annexed the state to its territories.

  • The Revolt of 1857

    • Rani was reluctant to rebel against the British. A massacre took place at Jhansi which made the sepoys left Jhansi, and Rani handled the administration. In 1858, when Jhansi was beseiged by Sir Hugh Rose, Rani defended Jhansi.
    Rani then fought for the independence along with Tatya Tope, Nana Sahib and Rao Sahib.

  • Death

    • On 18 June in Kotah-ki-Serai near the Phool Bagh of Gwalior, Rani with her commanded large Indian Force fought bravely against Captain Heneage and his squadron. But later, she got injured and died.

    Her tomb is in the Phool Bagh in Gwalior where she took her last breath.
